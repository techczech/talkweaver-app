HTML presentations

Single-file decks can still have a real presenter view.

One HTML file. A standalone deck, plus paired presenter and audience windows when needed.

Open the presenter window from the footer. From there, open the audience display when you are ready to move it to another screen.

The runtime is small but explicit.

  • Standalone clean browsing, hash links, overview, notes, and print mode.
  • Paired mode presenter controls a separate audience display window.
  • Modes reveal and focus are optional, not the default.

Everything is fully visible by default. Press R for reveal or F for focus to step through.

Presenter state is just serialised slide state.

Index, reveal count, font size, mode, and timestamp are enough for a robust first version.

Do not overfit the state model. Keep it small and derive previews, notes, labels, and hashes from slide metadata.

Reveal mode steps content in.

  • Arriving here auto-activates reveal (authored {mode=reveal}).
  • Each press of → adds the next point, softening the previous.
  • One more → shows the whole slide, then advances.

R turns the authored mode off. The next slide goes back to fully visible.

Quotes and figures participate too.

A presentation is a sequence of claims, each earning the next.
